Goodereads - App Redesign

Case Study Overview

Goodreads is a social cataloging app designed for book lovers to discover, track, and review books. It allows users to log and organize their reading by categorizing books as "Read," "Currently Reading," or "Want to Read." Based on reading history and ratings, the app provides personalized book recommendations. Users can also rate books on a 1-5 star scale and write reviews to help others decide what to read. Beyond tracking and reviewing, Goodreads fosters a sense of community through book clubs, discussion groups, and reading challenges. It also enables users to follow friends and favorite authors, keeping them updated on new releases, author Q&As, and literary events. Overall, Goodreads serves as a comprehensive platform for readers to explore books, connect with fellow book enthusiasts, and maintain a personal reading history.

Project Duration

February 2025 - Ongoing

Tools

  • Figma

Problem Statement

Goodreads users frequently report:

  • rating system is limited
  • search engine is vague
  • “Notes & Highlights” feature is only for Kindle
  • “More Actions” button is blank and confusing
  • lack of text editor for writing reviews



Goals

  • Add half star options to the rating system
  • Improve search: Add filter options to the search engine
  • Be more Inclusive: Change the “Notes & Highlights” section to allow manual entry for physical book readers
  • Enhance social features: Fix the “More Actions” button
  • Refine review page: Add a text editor to the review page

Design Process

User research

  • conducted surveys and interviews with active Goodreads users to understand pain points
  • analyzed app store reviews and competitor platforms
  • created personas representing different user types

Wireframing

  • developed lofi wireframes to test layout
  • created interactive prototype for usability test
  • iterated based on feedback, refining the navigation
